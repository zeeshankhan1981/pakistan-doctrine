Pakistan Under Siege

Pakistan is in the grip of a silent war. Since 2022, the military and intelligence agencies—ISI, MI, and their proxies—have waged a campaign of abductions, torture, and censorship across Balochistan, Sindh, KP, Gilgit-Baltistan, and Punjab. Thousands have vanished. Journalists are threatened, activists are killed, and entire regions are under siege. This site is a living archive of state violence and the cost of speaking out.

Featured Reports & Events

May 9th, 2023: The Crackdown

Following protests against military courts, over 5,000 people—including women, students, and lawyers—were detained across Punjab. Journalists reporting on the events were threatened and forced off air. The ISI and police raided homes in Lahore and Rawalpindi. Many remain missing.

Khuzdar Mass Graves, Balochistan

In January 2014, mass graves containing the remains of dozens of missing persons were discovered in Khuzdar. Families and activists accuse security forces of extrajudicial killings. Investigations were blocked, and no one was held accountable.

2024 Wheat Protests, Gilgit-Baltistan

In January 2024, thousands protested wheat shortages and government neglect. The military responded with arrests and violence. Activists and journalists covering the protests were detained or disappeared.

Jan Muhammad Mahar Assassinated, Sindh

In August 2023, prominent journalist Jan Muhammad Mahar was shot dead in Sukkur. His reporting on enforced disappearances and police abuses made him a target. The killers remain at large.

PTM Crackdown, KP

Leaders of the Pashtun Tahafuz Movement (PTM) have been jailed, attacked, or forced into hiding. Rallies in Waziristan and Swat are met with violence. Media coverage is banned, and activists live in constant fear of abduction.

Pakistan Under Siege: The Human Cost

Pakistan’s ongoing crisis is not just a matter of numbers—it is a story of families torn apart, communities living in fear, and a nation’s brightest minds forced to flee. Behind every data point is a human life: a student abducted, a journalist silenced, a protester jailed, a family struggling to survive economic collapse. These are not isolated incidents—they are the result of systemic repression, elite capture, and the unchecked power of the military establishment.

“They took my brother in the night. We still don’t know where he is. Every day, we wait for news, but none comes.”
— Sister of an enforced disappearance victim, Balochistan
