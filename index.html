May 9th, 2023

The Crackdown

Following protests against military courts, over 5,000 people—including women, students, and lawyers—were detained across Punjab. Journalists reporting on the events were threatened and forced off air.

January 2014

Khuzdar Mass Graves, Balochistan

Mass graves containing the remains of dozens of missing persons were discovered in Khuzdar. Families and activists accuse security forces of extrajudicial killings. Investigations were blocked, and no one was held accountable.

January 2024

Wheat Protests, Gilgit-Baltistan

Thousands protested wheat shortages and government neglect. The military responded with arrests and violence. Activists and journalists covering the protests were detained or disappeared.

Jan Muhammad Mahar Assassinated, Sindh

Prominent journalist Jan Muhammad Mahar was shot dead in Sukkur. His reporting on enforced disappearances and police abuses made him a target. The killers remain at large.

The Human Cost

Pakistan's ongoing crisis is not just a matter of numbers—it is a story of families torn apart, communities living in fear, and a nation's brightest minds forced to flee. Behind every data point is a human life: a student abducted, a journalist silenced, a protester jailed, a family struggling to survive economic collapse.

"They took my brother in the night. We still don't know where he is. Every day, we wait for news, but none comes."
— Sister of an enforced disappearance victim, Balochistan

These are not isolated incidents—they are the result of systemic repression, elite capture, and the unchecked power of the military establishment. The statistics and maps above are a call to action.
