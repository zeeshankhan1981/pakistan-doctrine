Imran Khan Doctrine: Truth, Justice, Freedom

Imran Khan, Pakistan’s former Prime Minister and the founder of PTI, has articulated a doctrine rooted in truth, justice, and freedom. His vision stands firmly against Islamophobia, supports the rights of Kashmiris and Palestinians, and calls for a just, sovereign, and inclusive Pakistan. This page highlights his doctrine with real data, international recognition, and global advocacy.

“Absolutely Not.”
— Imran Khan, rejecting U.S. military bases in Pakistan (June 2021, BBC)

Global Advocacy: Stand Against Islamophobia

  • UN General Assembly adopted Resolution A/RES/76/254 (2022) designating March 15 as the International Day to Combat Islamophobia, sponsored by Pakistan under Imran Khan’s leadership. (UN Press)
  • Imran Khan’s 2019 UNGA speech highlighted rising Islamophobia and called for global action. (Al Jazeera)

Justice for Kashmir & Palestine

  • Consistent calls for UN resolutions on Kashmir, condemning India’s revocation of Article 370 and military lockdown in 2019. (BBC)
  • Vocal support for Palestinian rights, condemning Israeli actions in Gaza and advocating for a two-state solution. (Al Jazeera)

Truth, Justice & Rule of Law

  • PTI government launched the Ehsaas Program, the largest social safety net in Pakistan’s history, benefiting 15 million families (World Bank).
  • Introduced Sehat Sahulat health cards, providing free healthcare to millions of Pakistanis. (Dawn)
  • Advocated for judicial independence and accountability, including public asset declarations and anti-corruption reforms. (Transparency International)

Leadership & International Recognition

  • Named among Time Magazine’s 100 Most Influential People (2019). (Time)
  • Recognized for global peace advocacy and humanitarian work. (Al Jazeera)
