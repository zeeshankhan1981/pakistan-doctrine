Imran Khan Doctrine: Truth, Justice, Freedom

Imran Khan, Pakistan’s former Prime Minister and the founder of PTI, has articulated a doctrine rooted in truth, justice, and freedom. His vision stands firmly against Islamophobia, supports the rights of Kashmiris and Palestinians, and calls for a just, sovereign, and inclusive Pakistan. This page highlights his doctrine with real data, international recognition, and global advocacy.

The Khan Doctrine: Key Principles

Sovereign Foreign Policy

"Absolutely Not" - refusal to compromise national sovereignty for foreign interests, including rejecting US bases in Pakistan post-Afghanistan withdrawal.

"Pakistan suffered 70,000 casualties in the last 20 years. We cannot afford any more military actions from our territory." - June 2021, Axios Interview

Islamic Welfare State

Creating a state modeled on Riyasat-e-Madina (the Prophet's state in Medina), founded on justice, compassion, and meritocracy. Implemented through Ehsaas Program, Sehat Cards, and social reforms.

"My vision is to create a welfare state, where the rule of law prevails, where the weak are uplifted as per the model of Riyasat-e-Madina." - August 2018, Victory Speech

Self-Determination for Kashmir

Staunch advocacy for Kashmiris' right to self-determination under UN resolutions. Internationalized the Kashmir issue after India's 2019 revocation of Article 370.

"Kashmir issue can only be resolved through dialogue and UN resolutions, not through military oppression." - September 2019, UNGA Speech

Rule of Law & Anti-Corruption

No society can progress without rule of law and accountability for the powerful. Implemented through asset recovery unit, independent prosecutions, and judicial reforms.

"A society that cannot uphold merit and hold the powerful accountable will never progress." - 2021

Palestine Rights

Unequivocal support for Palestinian rights and statehood. Rejected normalization with Israel without Palestinian statehood and advocated for a two-state solution.

"Our stance is very clear from day one and Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah had said Pakistan can never accept the state of Israel until the people of Palestine get rights and state." - August 2020

Climate Action

Billion Tree Tsunami project in KP, expanded to Ten Billion Tree Tsunami nationally. Global acknowledgment of Pakistan's leading role in reforestation and climate action.

"Pakistan may contribute less than 1% to global carbon emissions, but we are among the top ten countries most affected by climate change." - December 2019, UN Climate Summit

Digital Pakistan

Digital Pakistan program aimed to leverage technology for governance, transparency, and service delivery. Initiatives included digital payment systems, e-governance solutions, and support for technology startups.

"We are focusing on the technology sector to create employment for our tech-savvy youth. The Digital Pakistan initiative is crucial for Pakistan's economic future." - 2020

Global Impact & Relevance

Imran Khan's doctrine represents a significant attempt to reconcile Islamic values with modern governance, international relations, and social welfare. His independent foreign policy stance, advocacy for causes like Kashmir and Palestine, and emphasis on sovereignty have resonated across the Muslim world. While his domestic policies faced implementation challenges, his articulation of an Islamic welfare state has influenced political discourse in Pakistan and beyond.

His struggle for rule of law and against political persecution continues to inspire millions, making him one of the most consequential political figures in Pakistan's history and a significant voice in the Muslim world's political landscape.
