Khyber Pakhtunkhwa: The Frontline

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KP) faces ongoing violence, arbitrary detentions, and torture in police custody. In 2024, over 65 cases of human rights violations were reported, with many more likely unreported due to fear and insecurity. HRCP

Recent Incidents (2023–2024)

Arbitrary Detentions in Peshawar

On 25 March 2023, over 50 individuals were detained without charge during a security operation. Human rights groups raised concerns about due process violations. HRCP

Torture in Police Custody

On 10 May 2023, multiple reports of torture in police custody emerged. Human rights organizations documented severe physical abuse. Amnesty International

Detailed Research Findings: Human Rights in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (2016–2025)

Militant Attacks, Civilian Losses & Enforced Disappearances

  • 2016: Bacha Khan University massacre (20 killed), Shabqadar courthouse bombing (17 killed), widespread disappearances during security operations. Source
  • 2017: Mashal Khan lynched over blasphemy accusation; targeted killings of minorities. Source
  • 2018–2020: Disappearances of Pashtun activists; rise of PTM. Source
  • 2019: Kharqamar checkpoint incident—13 PTM protesters killed by military. Source
  • 2021: Peshawar mosque suicide bombing (100 killed), Karak Hindu temple rebuilt after mob attack. Source
  • 2023–2024: Resurgence of TTP, renewed attacks, civilian displacement. Source

Extrajudicial Killings, Military Abuses & Political Crackdown

  • 2016–2024: Security operations led to allegations of extrajudicial executions, collective punishment, and civilian deaths. Source
  • 2018: FATA merger into KP, first provincial elections in tribal areas, increased military presence. Source
  • 2019–2023: PTM leaders surveilled, arrested, and barred from travel. Source

Religious & Minority Persecution

  • 2017: Mashal Khan lynching; targeted killings of Sikhs, Hindus, and Ahmadis. Source
  • 2020: Karak Hindu temple attacked by mob. Source
  • 2021–2024: Targeted attacks on minorities, blasphemy accusations, and mob violence. Source

Media Suppression & Civil Society

  • 2016–2024: Journalists threatened by both militants and state actors for coverage of PTM, abuses, and militant threats. Source
  • 2021: Crackdown on social media activists in KP and FATA. Source

Timeline of Major Incidents

  • 2016: Security operations and militant attacks continued; enforced disappearances of political activists. Source
  • 2017: Mashal Khan lynched in KP over false blasphemy accusation. Source
  • 2018-20: Disappearances of Pashtun activists; rise of PTM in response to extrajudicial killings (Naqeebullah Mehsud case). Source
  • 2021: Karak Hindu temple rebuilt after mob attack; targeted killings of minorities and activists. Source
  • 2023: Peshawar mosque bombing killed 100 police officers; TTP resurgence. Source

Key Statistics

  • 2,942 missing persons cases registered in KP by end-2020 (highest of any province).
  • Dozens of targeted killings of minorities in KP in recent years.
  • January 2023: Peshawar mosque bombing killed 100 people.
  • PTM protests and activism surged after Naqeebullah Mehsud’s killing.

International Responses

  • UN and U.S. condemned Peshawar mosque bombing and attacks on minorities.
  • UN Working Group on Enforced Disappearances pressed Pakistan for action in KP.
  • U.S. and EU called for protection of minorities and end to enforced disappearances.
