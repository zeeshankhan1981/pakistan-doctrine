Balochistan: Under Siege

Balochistan remains the epicenter of enforced disappearances, extrajudicial killings, and military operations in Pakistan. In 2024, over 150 cases of enforced disappearances and dozens of extrajudicial killings were reported, with countless families still searching for answers. (HRCP)

Detailed Research Findings: Human Rights in Balochistan (2016–2025)

Enforced Disappearances & Extrajudicial Killings

  • 2016: 728 officially recorded disappearances (likely undercounted); "kill-and-dump" policy by security forces. Source 1, Source 2
  • 2017: 868 new disappearance cases; bullet-riddled corpse of Nazar Mohammad Baloch found in Turbat. Source
  • 2018: Record surge—1,098 new disappearances; Awaran "encounter" killings. Source
  • 2019: 800 new disappearances; Sammi Baloch's protest march for her missing father. Source
  • 2020: 415 new cases; Sohail Baloch and Faisal Marri abducted from Quetta campus. Source
  • 2021: 952 disappearances by April; Machh massacre—11 Hazara Shia miners executed by ISIS. Source
  • 2023: 885 new disappearance cases. Supreme Court resumes hearings. Source

Militant Attacks & Civilian Casualties

  • 2016: Quetta Civil Hospital bombing (~70 dead, mostly lawyers), IS & Jamaat-ul-Ahrar claimed responsibility. Source
  • 2017: BLA attack near Gwadar killed 10 Punjabi laborers. Source
  • 2018: Mastung election rally bombing—149 killed. Source
  • 2019: Pearl Continental Hotel attack in Gwadar; Hazarganji market bombing (20 dead, 8 Hazara Shias). Source
  • 2021: Machh massacre; ongoing Hazara killings. Source

Media Suppression & Civil Society

  • 2016: Journalists killed or missing; Balochistan among deadliest regions for press. Source
  • 2020: HRCP: "Even a pandemic did not slow the epidemic of enforced disappearances." Source
  • 2021–2024: Journalists, activists, and NGOs face threats, surveillance, and violence. Source

Religious & Sectarian Persecution

  • 2017–2021: Hazara Shia community targeted in Quetta and Machh; systematic persecution. Source
  • 2018: Hazara killings prompt week-long sit-in; Army Chief pledges security. Source
  • 2021: ISIS massacre of Hazara miners in Machh. Source

Recent Incidents (2023–2024)

Category Breakdown

Timeline of Major Incidents

  • 2016: Hundreds of enforced disappearances (728 officially recorded), many from Balochistan. Kill-and-dump policy by security forces. Major suicide bombing at Quetta's Civil Hospital killed ~70 people. Source
  • 2017: 868 new disappearance cases, many in Balochistan. BLA attack near Gwadar killed 10 Punjabi laborers. Source
  • 2020: Hayat Baloch, a student, shot dead by FC soldier in Turbat. Court-martial and rare conviction. Source
  • 2021: Machh massacre: 11 Hazara Shia coal miners executed by ISIS. Surge in enforced disappearances (952 cases by April). Source
  • 2022: Major attacks by BLA on FC bases; train derailment in Bolan. Journalist Athar Mateen killed in Quetta. Source
  • 2023-24: 885 new disappearance cases in 2023. Supreme Court resumes hearings on disappearances. Source

Key Statistics

  • Over 9,000 disappearance cases nationwide since 2011; ~2,000 unresolved as of 2024. Balochistan has the highest share.
  • Hundreds of extrajudicial killings and militant attacks reported annually in Balochistan.
  • Machh massacre (2021): 11 Hazara Shia miners killed.
  • 2021: 952 disappearances by April; 692 in Balochistan in March alone.

International Responses

  • UN, EU, and US have repeatedly condemned enforced disappearances and extrajudicial killings in Balochistan.
  • UN Secretary-General condemned Machh massacre; Amnesty International called for systemic reforms.
  • 2023: UN Committee Against Torture and EU tied human rights to Pakistan's trade status.
  • 2024: Supreme Court called enforced disappearance "a blemish on the rule of law."
