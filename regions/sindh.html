Sindh: Struggle for Rights

Sindh has witnessed a surge in crackdowns on peaceful protests, police brutality, and violations of freedom of assembly. In 2024, dozens of cases of arbitrary detention and excessive force were reported, especially in Karachi and Hyderabad. (Amnesty)

Recent Incidents (2023–2024)

Crackdown on Protesters in Karachi

On 10 June 2023, security forces detained 45 protesters during a peaceful demonstration against electricity shortages. Multiple injuries were reported. Amnesty International

Police Brutality in Hyderabad

On 15 July 2023, two civilians were killed during a police operation. Local human rights groups reported excessive use of force. HRW

Detailed Research Findings: Human Rights in Sindh (2016–2025)

Police Encounter Killings & Enforced Disappearances

  • 2016: Karachi police killed 318 suspects in raids (many fake encounters). MQM leader's speech incited attacks on media. Source
  • 2018: Killing of Naqeebullah Mehsud by SSP Rao Anwar, who was later sanctioned by the US for 190+ fake encounters. Source
  • 2020: 99 enforced disappearances of Sindhi/Mohajir activists (VMPS); 47 in 2021. Source
  • 2019: Body of Sindhi activist Niaz Lashari found in Karachi after abduction in Balochistan. Source

Religious Minorities, Forced Conversions & Mob Violence

  • 2019: 7 cases of forced conversion of Hindu girls (Umerkot/Thar); 31 in 2020. Source
  • 2020: 586 blasphemy charges nationwide, several in Sindh. Source
  • 2021: Muslim physician lynched by mob in Kotri; Hindu businessman arrested for blasphemy in Mirpurkhas. Source
  • 2019: Ghotki anti-Hindu violence after false blasphemy accusation; mob attacks on temples/homes. Source

Political Crackdown, Arbitrary Arrests & Civil Society

  • 2022–2023: Hundreds of PTI and nationalist activists detained under preventive detention laws after unrest. Source
  • 2016–2024: Journalists and activists face intimidation, abductions, and self-censorship. Sindh Journalists and Media Professionals Protection Act passed in 2022. Source
  • 2021: Press freedom hero Ahfaz-ur-Rahman dies; Sindh journalists vow to resist censorship. Source
  • 2022: Floods disproportionately affected minorities; discrimination in relief. Source

Timeline of Major Incidents

  • 2016: Karachi police reported killing 318 suspects in raids; many were fake encounters. MQM leader's speech incited attacks on media houses. Security forces intervened after ARY News was attacked by mobs. Forced conversions of Hindu girls continued, with local NGOs documenting cases. Sindh Assembly passed a bill criminalizing forced conversion, but enforcement was stalled by religious parties. Source
  • 2017: Mob lynching in Hyderabad after blasphemy accusation; forced conversions of Hindu girls continued. Police barely managed to disperse mobs attacking Ahmadi worship places and Hindu teenagers were jailed on blasphemy charges. International monitors, including the EU, flagged the alarming increase in blasphemy accusations. Source
  • 2018: Naqeebullah Mehsud, aspiring model, killed in a staged encounter by Karachi police. US sanctioned SSP Rao Anwar for over 190 fake police encounters resulting in 400+ deaths. Dozens of Sindhi activists disappeared; JSMM reported 10 members vanished. Source
  • 2019: Ghotki: Mob ransacked Hindu temple after false blasphemy accusation. At least 7 cases of Hindu girls' forced conversion in Umerkot and Thar. US State Dept cited Ghotki attack and lack of minority protection. Source
  • 2020: 99 enforced disappearances of Sindhi activists (VMPS). Tortured body of activist Niaz Lashari found in Karachi. Police killings: Irfan Jatoi shot dead in Shikarpur in a staged encounter. Blasphemy cases spiked, with at least 586 charged nationwide. Source
  • 2021: 47 Sindhi persons disappeared. Mob lynching in Kotri after blasphemy accusation. Journalist Nazim Jokhio abducted and murdered. Sindh Assembly protested abduction of political workers by federal agencies. Source
  • 2022: Sindh passed Journalists and Media Professionals Protection Act. Civil society felt slightly freer after years of crackdowns, but activists still faced surveillance. Source
  • 2023–2024: Judicial review of fake encounters and custodial torture. Sindh High Court reopened Naqeebullah case. International engagement: UN UPR review, EU linked trade benefits to human rights progress. Source

Key Statistics

  • 318 police encounter killings in Karachi in 2016 alone; dozens annually since.
  • Dozens of enforced disappearances of Sindhi and Mohajir activists each year; 99 in 2020, 47 in 2021 (VMPS).
  • At least 7 cases of Hindu girls' forced conversion in Umerkot and Thar in 2019; 31 cases of forced conversion documented in 2020 (HRCP).
  • 586 people charged with blasphemy in 2020 nationwide (CSJ report), with Sindh and Punjab most affected.
  • 2,942 missing persons cases in KP by end-2020, many activists from Sindh.

International Responses

  • US sanctioned Karachi police officer Rao Anwar for human rights abuses (2019).
  • EU Parliament and US Commission on International Religious Freedom flagged forced conversions in Sindh and lack of minority protection.
  • UN Working Group on Enforced Disappearances cited Sindh cases in annual reports; UN UPR review in 2023 highlighted disappearances and blasphemy law abuses.
  • HRCP, Amnesty, and international NGOs repeatedly condemned police brutality, disappearances, and anti-minority violence in Sindh.
  • EU linked Pakistan's GSP+ trade benefits to progress on human rights, including reforms in Sindh.
