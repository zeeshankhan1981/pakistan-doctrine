Gilgit-Baltistan: Silenced Voices

Gilgit-Baltistan (GB) has seen a rise in restrictions on freedom of expression and forced displacement. In 2024, at least 35 cases of rights violations were reported, with journalists and activists facing detention and internet shutdowns. HRW

Recent Incidents (2023–2024)

Freedom of Expression Restrictions

On 5 April 2023, multiple journalists and activists were detained for social media posts. Reports of internet shutdowns during protests. HRW

Forced Displacement in Skardu

On 28 June 2023, hundreds of families were forcibly displaced due to military operations. Reports of property destruction. HRCP

Detailed Research Findings: Human Rights in Gilgit-Baltistan (2016–2025)

Suppression of Political Rights & Freedom of Expression

  • 2016–2024: GB excluded from constitutional framework, denied provincial status and National Assembly representation. Protests for rights met with arrests and intimidation. Source
  • 2016–2024: Journalists, activists, and students detained for social media posts and criticism of authorities. Source
  • 2023–2024: At least 35 cases of rights violations, including internet shutdowns and anti-terror laws used to silence dissent. Source

Forced Displacement, Land Rights & Sectarian Tensions

  • 2016–2024: Military operations in Skardu and Diamer led to forced displacement, property destruction, and protests over land seizures. Source
  • 2022–2024: HRCP and Amnesty raised alarm over forced displacement and curbs on dissent. Source
  • 2016–2024: Periodic sectarian violence, especially targeting Shia communities. Authorities imposed curfews and restricted processions, citing security. Source

Suppression of Civil Society, Education & Language Rights

  • 2016–2024: NGOs and rights defenders harassed, denied registration, and restricted from peaceful assembly. Source
  • 2016–2024: Movements for mother-tongue education and local history in curricula face official resistance. Source

Timeline of Major Incidents

  • 2016-2020: Crackdown on freedom of expression; arrests for social media posts; forced displacement in Skardu. Source
  • 2021: Internet shutdowns and arrests of journalists for online speech. Source
  • 2022: HRCP and Amnesty raised alarm over forced displacement and curbs on dissent. Source

Key Statistics

  • Multiple internet shutdowns and dozens of arrests for online speech in recent years.
  • Dozens of families forcibly displaced in Skardu and other areas.
  • GB journalists and activists frequently targeted for dissent.

International Responses

  • HRW and HRCP condemned restrictions on expression and forced displacement in GB.
  • Amnesty International and UN agencies called for protection of journalists and activists.
