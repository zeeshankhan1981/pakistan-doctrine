Punjab: Rights and Repression

Punjab, Pakistan's most populous province, has seen a spike in extrajudicial killings and restrictions on religious freedom. In 2024, over 120 cases of human rights violations were reported, with minorities and activists at particular risk. Amnesty

Recent Incidents (2023–2024)

Extrajudicial Killings in Lahore

On 15 January 2023, four civilians were killed in a police encounter. Human rights groups questioned the circumstances of the killings. Amnesty International

Freedom of Religion Restrictions

On 1 March 2023, multiple religious minorities were detained under blasphemy laws. Reports of harassment and intimidation. HRW

Detailed Research Findings: Human Rights in Punjab (2016–2025)

Blasphemy Laws, Mob Violence & Religious Persecution

  • 2016–2024: Punjab saw the highest number of blasphemy accusations and mob violence in Pakistan. Source
  • 2021: Lynching of Sri Lankan factory manager Priyantha Kumara in Sialkot. Source
  • 2022: Stoning of Mushtaq Ahmed in Khanewal for alleged blasphemy. Source
  • 2023: Jaranwala pogrom—mobs burned 26 churches and dozens of Christian homes. Source
  • 2018: Aasia Bibi acquitted after 8 years on death row for blasphemy; sparked nationwide protests. Source

Extrajudicial Killings, Police Abuses & Enforced Disappearances

  • 2019: Sahiwal incident—family killed in police encounter. Source
  • 2023: Crackdown after Imran Khan’s arrest—thousands of arbitrary detentions, use of military courts for civilians. Source
  • 2023–2024: Over 120 cases of human rights violations reported, including extrajudicial killings in Lahore. Source

Suppression of Media & Civil Society

  • 2016–2024: Journalists and activists in Punjab faced intimidation, abduction, and violence, especially during election periods and protests. Source

Timeline of Major Incidents

  • 2018: Aasia Bibi acquitted of blasphemy after 8 years on death row; nationwide protests. Source
  • 2021: Mob demolished Ahmadi mosque in Sialkot; anti-minority violence continued. Source
  • 2023: Religious freedom and extrajudicial killings remain major issues. Source

Key Statistics

  • Hundreds of blasphemy accusations annually; Punjab sees highest number of cases.
  • Punjab is home to most of Pakistan’s religious minorities and blasphemy-related violence.
  • 2018: Aasia Bibi acquitted after 8 years on death row for blasphemy.

International Responses

  • U.S., EU, and Canada praised Aasia Bibi’s acquittal; Canada granted her asylum.
  • EU Parliament and U.S. State Department highlighted anti-minority violence and blasphemy cases in Punjab.
  • UN Working Group on Enforced Disappearances raised Punjab cases in annual reports.
