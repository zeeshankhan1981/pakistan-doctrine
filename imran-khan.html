Early Life & Cricketing Legend

Born in 1952 in Lahore, Imran Khan was educated at Aitchison College, Lahore, and later at Oxford University. He led Pakistan to its first Cricket World Cup victory in 1992, becoming a national hero and global sports icon.

“Compromise for your dream, but never compromise on your dream.”
— Imran Khan

Source: ESPN Cricinfo

Philanthropy & Service

After retiring from cricket, Imran Khan founded the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital & Research Centre, Pakistan’s first charity cancer hospital, and Namal University, providing quality education in rural Punjab.

  • Shaukat Khanum Hospital (est. 1994): Treated over 75% of patients free of cost. (Official Site)
  • Namal University (est. 2008): Bringing world-class education to underprivileged youth. (Official Site)

Political Struggle & Vision

Imran Khan founded Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) in 1996, facing decades of political adversity and ridicule. His unwavering commitment to justice, anti-corruption, and the rule of law resonated with millions. In 2018, he became the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan, championing social protection programs and advocating for an independent foreign policy.

  • Ehsaas Program: Direct relief to 15+ million families. (Ehsaas Portal)
  • Sehat Sahulat: Universal health coverage for 27 million households. (Social Protection)
  • TIME 100 Most Influential People (2019). (TIME)
“You only lose when you give up.”
— Imran Khan

Philosophy: Justice, Determination, and Service

Imran Khan’s philosophy is rooted in justice, compassion, and resilience. He believes in empowering the marginalized, upholding the rule of law, and serving Pakistan with integrity. His determination in the face of adversity has inspired generations.

“My mission is to make Pakistan a country where justice and compassion prevail, and every citizen has dignity.”
— Imran Khan, 2023
